Commercial Fire Risk Assessment

Every commercial premises
requires a valid assessment.

The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 applies to virtually every commercial premises in England and Wales — from a single-room office to a large industrial complex. There is no minimum size threshold.

Under Article 9 of the Fire Safety Order, the responsible person — typically the employer, building owner or occupier — must ensure a suitable and sufficient fire risk assessment is carried out by a competent person and implemented. The assessment must be reviewed whenever premises, processes or occupancy changes.

A commercial fire risk assessment covers the identification of fire hazards, the identification of persons at risk, the evaluation of existing fire precautions, and a written report with prioritised recommendations. For most commercial premises, the on-site inspection takes between one and four hours.

The Responsible Person
"Where the employer is not the owner of the premises, the employer must co-operate with the owner of the premises to enable them to comply with the requirements of the Order."

Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005. Both the employer and the building owner may carry duties under the Order. FireCertificates advises on which applies to your specific situation.

Common Questions

Questions answered.

Do I need a fire risk assessment for a small office?+
Yes. The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 applies to all non-domestic premises regardless of size. A single-room office with one employee requires a fire risk assessment. There is no minimum threshold.
Who is the responsible person for a commercial fire risk assessment?+
The responsible person is typically the employer (if you employ people in the premises), the building owner or the occupier. In multi-occupied buildings, both the tenant employer and the landlord may carry duties. FireCertificates can advise on your specific situation.
How often does a commercial fire risk assessment need reviewing?+
At minimum, annually for most commercial premises. A review is also required after any change to the premises, processes, occupancy or use. An enforcement officer may treat an out-of-date assessment as no assessment at all.
What happens if I don't have a fire risk assessment for my commercial premises?+
Enforcement notices, prohibition orders, unlimited fines and prosecution are all possible outcomes of non-compliance with the Fire Safety Order. Local fire and rescue services carry out inspections and can issue improvement notices requiring compliance within specific timescales.
