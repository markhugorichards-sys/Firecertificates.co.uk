Understanding Your Obligations

What is a fire risk assessment?

A fire risk assessment is a systematic and thorough evaluation of your premises from the point of view of fire safety. It is a legal requirement for any non-domestic premises in England and Wales — including commercial properties, HMOs, offices, warehouses, and any building where employees or the public are present.

Under the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005, the responsible person must carry out a suitable and sufficient fire risk assessment and implement any improvements identified. Failing to do so can result in enforcement notices, prohibition orders, unlimited fines, or imprisonment.

Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 — Article 9
"The responsible person must make a suitable and sufficient assessment of the risks to which relevant persons are exposed for the purpose of identifying the general fire precautions they need to take."

Applies to all non-domestic premises in England and Wales. FireCertificates connects you with a certified assessor across Hampshire, Surrey and Berkshire.

Common Questions

Fire risk assessment questions.

Do I legally need a fire risk assessment for my commercial property? +
Yes. The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 requires the responsible person for any non-domestic premises to carry out a suitable and sufficient fire risk assessment. This applies to offices, warehouses, retail units, restaurants, community halls and any property where employees or members of the public are present. There is no de minimis threshold — if it is a non-domestic premises, an assessment is required. Contact FireCertificates for a free assessment of your specific situation.
Is a fire risk assessment required for an HMO? +
Yes. All Houses in Multiple Occupation require a fire risk assessment. For mandatory licensing under the Housing Act 2004 and the Licensing of Houses in Multiple Occupation (Prescribed Descriptions) (England) Order 2018, a valid fire risk assessment is a core requirement. Local authorities may impose additional HMO conditions. FireCertificates connects you with an assessor experienced in HMO assessments across Hampshire, Surrey and Berkshire.
How often does a fire risk assessment need to be reviewed? +
A fire risk assessment must be kept up to date. The Fire Safety Order requires it to be reviewed whenever there is reason to suspect it is no longer valid — including changes to premises, occupancy, use, new hazards or any fire safety incident. In practice, most assessors recommend a formal review every 12 months for commercial premises and HMOs. An out-of-date assessment can be treated as no assessment at all by enforcement officers.
What qualifications should a fire risk assessor hold? +
The assessment must be carried out by a competent person. While there is no single mandatory qualification, BAFE SP205 registration and membership of the Institution of Fire Engineers are the recognised industry benchmarks. FireCertificates only connects clients with assessors holding recognised professional qualifications who carry appropriate professional indemnity insurance.
What happens if I receive a fire enforcement notice? +
A fire enforcement notice from your local Fire and Rescue Service requires urgent action. Depending on the notice type, you may have as little as 28 days to comply, and in the case of a prohibition order, use of all or part of the premises may be immediately restricted. FireCertificates can arrange an emergency assessment and connect you with an assessor experienced in responding to enforcement action. Contact us immediately.
How long does a fire risk assessment take and what does it cover? +
Assessment duration depends on the size and complexity of the premises. A standard small commercial office or HMO typically takes one to two hours on site. A larger warehouse, pub or multi-floor office may take three to four hours. The assessment covers identification of fire hazards, identification of persons at risk, evaluation of existing precautions, recommendations for improvement, and a written report with prioritised action points. The report is usually delivered within one to three working days of the site visit.
Is FireCertificates a fire safety company? +
No. FireCertificates is a specialist connection service. We are not fire risk assessors and do not carry out assessments ourselves. We connect property owners and managers with independent, BAFE SP205 or IFE-registered fire risk assessors who carry full professional indemnity insurance. All assessment work is carried out under the assessor's own terms of engagement. Call for a free, no-obligation assessment of your requirements.
