HMO Fire Risk Assessment Requirements

Why HMOs require a
specialist fire risk assessment.

HMOs present specific fire safety challenges that standard commercial assessments do not fully address. A fire risk assessment for an HMO must comply with BS 9792:2025 and the specific requirements of your local authority licensing scheme.

A House in Multiple Occupation is defined as a property let to three or more unrelated tenants who share facilities such as a kitchen or bathroom. All HMOs are subject to the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 for communal areas, and the Housing Act 2004 for the overall property fire safety requirements.

For mandatory HMO licensing — which applies to properties of five or more tenants in two or more households — a valid fire risk assessment is a core licensing condition. Many local authorities also apply additional licensing schemes requiring fire risk assessments for smaller HMOs.

HMO Legal Framework
"A fire risk assessment is required for all Houses in Multiple Occupation under both the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 and the Housing Act 2004."

The 2025 British Standard BS 9792:2025 sets the current benchmark for fire risk assessments in sleeping accommodation including HMOs. Local authority licensing officers inspect against this standard.

What an HMO fire risk assessment covers

Fire hazard identification — means of escape evaluation — fire detection and alarm systems — fire door condition and compliance — emergency lighting — fire suppression equipment — sleeping risk assessment — written report with prioritised action plan.

Common Questions

Questions answered.

Is a fire risk assessment required for every HMO?+
Yes. All HMOs require a fire risk assessment under both the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 (for communal areas) and the Housing Act 2004 (for overall property safety). For mandatory HMO licensing — five or more tenants in two or more households — a valid fire risk assessment is a core licensing condition. Many local authorities also require it for smaller HMOs under additional licensing schemes.
What is BS 9792:2025 and does my HMO assessment need to comply?+
BS 9792:2025 is the current British Standard for fire risk assessments in premises providing sleeping accommodation, including HMOs. Local authority licensing officers inspect HMO fire risk assessments against this standard. Assessors in the FireCertificates network produce BS 9792:2025-compliant reports.
How long does an HMO fire risk assessment take?+
Most HMO assessments take one to two hours on site for a standard three to four bedroom property. Larger or more complex properties may take two to four hours. The written report is typically delivered within one to three working days of the site visit.
My HMO licence application is due soon — how quickly can you arrange an assessment?+
FireCertificates confirms a certified assessor within 24 hours of your enquiry. Contact us with your deadline and we will match you with an assessor who can accommodate your timeline.
Does a cheap HMO fire risk assessment meet the standard required by the council?+
Not always. A £99 desktop pro-forma assessment is often rejected by local authority licensing officers. Your assessment must be carried out by a competent person on site and must comply with BS 9792:2025. FireCertificates only works with BAFE SP205 or IFE-registered assessors who produce compliant, on-site assessments.
